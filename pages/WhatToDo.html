To prevent IPV, improving the level of security of devices is essential.

Set up a password for your device that only you know.

1. Avoid: birth dates (0120, if your birthday is January 20th), simple series of number(1234, 4321) repetitions. (0000, 1111)

2. Log out of accounts and apps so that if others do gain access to the device, they can't get the content in the apps.

3. Be aware of who has access to your device and watch out for any unfamiliar application in your phone that you didn't install.

4. Protect the device with antivirus/antimalware software, althuogh they aren’t aiming for spy apps, they still doing a great job for about half of the malicious apps.

5. Constantly check your device about settings and applications, especially for those require location, read/write, and sending message authority.

Having others gain physically access to your mobile device always make you vulnerable to IPV no matter how strong or unique your password is. Therefore, keeping possession of your mobile devices at all times is the best way to protect your privacy while you're in a intimate relationship.
